A World of Angels and Demons

By Fei

Chapter 1

It was anarchy high’s annual Christmas party.

Everyone was dancing and talking to each other.

Everyone except Marin Brooklyn.

He was sitting in the back, phone in hand as he tried not to panic at the sheer amount of people touching him.

The school gym had been converted into something between a ballroom and a disco ball. And the air was relatively warm compared to the snow falling just outside the venue.

Soon, Marin felt his chest tightening.

‘No, no, no…not now…’ Marin thought, before going over quickly to the back exit.

Once he was outside, he took out his inhaler from his coat pocket and with shaky hands, took a few puffs.

As he was catching his breath, the exit door swung open, and someone stepped out.

A werewolf.

Tall, tan, brown hair and eyes, with alert ears and a tail which swished lazily.

The person had a lit cigarette, then looked at Marin.

The person’s eyes scanned Marin from top to bottom, then landed on his inhaler.

Without hesitation, the person dropped his cigarette and crushed it under his boot.

Marin was slightly confused “…why’d you do that…?”

The guy just looked at him and said “i don’t want anyone to choke because of me. That includes people with asthma”

“O-oh…thank you…” Marin replied

“You don’t have to thank me for not being a jerk” the guy said, leaning against the wall.

An awkward silence fell over them.

Marin knew who this guy was.

Everyone did.

Who wouldn’t know Aiden rondava?

One of the most popular students in the whole school.

Marin didn’t hate nor like him.

Probably because they’d never spoken before-

“You smell like sadness” Aiden then blurted out.

“Pardon?” Marin asked, French accent slipping through his lips.

“Yeah…you smell like rain, and old books. And there’s a hint of tears in your scent too” Aiden said

Marin suddenly remembered werewolves had a superior sense of scent.

“…” Marin didn’t reply. He couldn’t. He didn’t know what to say.

Aiden sensed this, and changed the topic

“How come I’ve never seen you before?” Aiden said. “You’re pretty noticeable”

And Aiden was right. Marin was basically albino, with fluffy white hair, pale skin that glowed slightly under the snowy moonlight and purple-amethyst eyes.

“…I prefer not to be noticed. It makes me uncomfortable when people stare” Marin replied quietly

“I get it. But you’re kinda cute though. It’s a shame people don’t notice” Aiden said ever-so-casually.

“…you have a lot of confidence. I wish I did.” Marin admitted bitterly.

“I wouldn’t call it ‘confidence’, although I do have it. It’s just my ADHD making me voice out every single impulsive thought I have” Aiden replied honestly

“…oh…I see…” marin said.

After a few minutes of talking, Marin figured he should head back inside, then head home.

“I need to leave. It’s getting late.” Marin said, glancing at the time on his phone.

10:25PM.

Heather would kill him if he wasn’t back by 10:30.

“Awh…so soon? I was starting to like talking to you…” Aiden said, ears drooping slightly.

“I’m sorry…but I really need to go. I have a curfew-“ Marin said, before he sneezed thrice.

Aiden just walked over to him, took off his own scarf and wrapped it around Marin’s neck. “Here. Before you catch a cold.”

“…thank you.” Marin said, his cheeks starting to flush pink. No one’s ever been this nice to him befor

“I should probably get your number” Aiden said with a smirk “so we can talk about the scarf”

“Yeah…about the scarf” Marin said, a small smile on his face as he took Aiden’s phone and typed in this number before returning it.

“See you later?” Aiden said.

“Probably” Marin said.

As Marin’s silhouette got smaller and faded into the night, Aiden couldn’t help but wonder…

‘Why do I feel like there’s something off about him…?’

He pushed the feeling aside temporarily, and went back inside, where the party was.

As Marin got to his front door, he checked the time.

10:31 PM.

He didn’t want to go in.

But he had to.

He took out his key to the duplex and shakily unlocked the front door.

As he stepped in, he saw her.

Heather.

His stepmother.

She was sitting in the living room, a glass of alcohol in her hand.

She turned to look at him.

“You’re late. It’s 10:31.” She said coldly.

“…I’m sorry, Heather…” he replied, head hung low in fear.

“Maybe I should stop you from going to all these damn parties. Then you’ll be grateful.” Heather said through gritted teeth

Marin stayed quiet. He knew better than to talk too early.

“I still have to discipline you for being late. I wonder how…” Heather said, as she stood up.

Marin flinched at the word ‘discipline’

He knew what that meant.

As she got up, Marin looked at her other hand.

His heart dropped.

She had her whip.

By the time she was done with him, his upper body was sore, and he had small cuts in his skin too.

He didn’t cry

Not in front of her.

He waited till he was in his room.

Then he broke down.

Tears started streaming down his face

Sobs muffled in Aiden’s scarf.

The pain was unbearable.

Yet he’d tolerated it for about 6 years.

He was still wearing his shirt, but his coat was long forgotten downstairs

He slowly took off his shirt in front of the mirror.

He had a good physique, yes.

He could’ve been mistaken for a model

If not for the scars that littered his upper body. From his collarbone to his waist.

Some had faded over time.

Some were still fresh

Some were still bleeding.

He stripped himself bare and trudged over to the bathroom in his room.

He filled the bathtub with warm water, and put on gloves before stepping in.

He wore the gloves because water clashed with his fire powers and caused a reaction of smoke which left Marin’s lungs fighting for air, and his palms bright red as though he’d experienced a second-degree burn

He winced as he lowered himself into the tub.

He hoped the water would stop the bleeding.

It did, but the emotional weight lingered slightly.

By the time he got out of the tub, the water was tinted slightly red and had a faint metallic scent.

He dried himself off, put on his pajamas which covered nearly every inch of his skin, and crawled into bed.

He winced as he laid down on the soft mattress, pulling the covers up to his chin.

He couldn’t sleep, so he decided to scroll on his phone to pass time.

As he scrolled through social media, a text came in

‘Sup. Party just ended. Too bad you weren’t there tho. Now I’ve gotta get home because I promised to be back at midnight. BRB in about 10 mins’

Marin didn’t need to be a genius to know who it was.

Aiden.
