A World of Angels and Demons

By Fei

Chapter 3

By the time it was lunch period, he was praying he wouldn’t run into Aiden

He went to a secluded part of the school and looked out the frosted windows

At that section of the school, it was pretty cold, and Marin could practically see his own breath

He didn’t bring any food to school with him today, and he forgot to take lunch money from the safe

“… I guess I’ll starve…” Marin muttered to himself

“Starve, huh?“ Said a familiar voice.

Marin flinched. Looked up.

And there stood Aiden , leaning against the wall with a cocky smirk on his face.

“… How long have you been standing there…?” Marin asked, avoiding eye contact.

“Not too long. A certain look alike of mine told me he ran into someone who was ‘awkwardly confusing me with him, and looking extremely nervous‘. I knew it was you, but I didn’t think you were ignoring me” Aiden said

“N-No, I’m not ignoring you, I just… Needed time to myself” Marin said

At that moment, Marin’s stomach growled

Marin was embarrassed, a blush creeping up his neck to the tips of his ears

Aiden, on the other hand, was amused “you seem hungry” he said

“I-I’m not-“ Marin started, but another growl from his stomach interrupted him. “OK, maybe just a little bit”

“As I recall, you said you’d ‘starve’. Don’t you have any lunch money?” Aiden inquired.

“No… I don’t” Marin said after a bit of thought

“Then I’ll buy you lunch. come on“ Aiden said, guessing from Marin to get up

“… No, no it’s fine. I can manage. You really don’t have to worry-“ marin tried to weasel his way out of the situation, but Aiden was persistent

“You’re thin. Do you eat properly? How much do you weigh?” Aiden asked, grabbing Marin’s wrist as he stepped closer.

Marin was on high alert. If Aiden kept on poking around, he’d see the scars.

“Being thin is part of the family genetics. It’s just how I am, OK? You don’t need to worry about something you barely know” Marin said, yanking his hand free from Aiden’s grip.

“… You’re being kind of defensive. Did I do something wrong? All I wanted to do was to buy you lunch… ” Aiden said, stepping back a bit

“… You didn’t do anything wrong, it’s just… I… prefer to eat alone”

“But you have literally nothing to eat”

“I’ll manage”

Aiden was genuinely concerned. He could tell Marin was hiding something.

He just couldn’t put a finger on it

“…hold on…where’s my scarf…?” Aiden asked, arching an eyebrow

“…o-oh…uh…I…sort of…forget it at home…?” Marin replied nervously. He was expecting an earful.

“Oh, that’s fine. You can keep it if you want to, I have a lot of other scarves”

“No, no, I can’t keep it, I’ll return it tomorrow and-“

“Nah, you can keep it. But if you really don’t want it, you can return it. I’m not gonna force you”

“Oh…ok…I’m returning it…”

“Alright then”

Then it was back to awkward silence

The bell rang.

Lunch was over.

“I should go…” Marin said, getting up

“Oh, ok…see you around” Aiden said

Marin got up, and as he was about to leave, he said

“Thank you for caring, Aiden.”

Then he left.

As Marin left, that feeling creeped up on Aiden again.

‘Something’s off…no…something’s WRONG…’

Later that afternoon…

School was over.

Students were beginning to leave school in their various cliques.

Marin stayed back, helping out in the library.

Eventually, he sat down at a table and began to read

Someone took a seat opposite him. He didn’t pay any attention to it at first, until…

“Marin?”

Marin looked up.

Before he spoke, he took a good look at the person, then replied

“…can I help you, Ethan?”

“Yeah…um..Aiden told me to give you this” Ethan said, before passing a strawberry milk boba tea along with the straw across the table towards Marin

Marin was stunned. He wanted to decline, but he figured it’d be rude.

He slowly put his book down and took the boba tea.

“…tell him I said thanks. Strawberry’s my favorite…” Marin said, before putting the straw in and began to take a sip.

It was delicious.

But Marin wasn’t used to such kindness.

“What are you reading?” Ethan asked

“Oh, just something from the fantasy section” Marin replied

“It’s interesting to learn about other species, isn’t it?” Ethan said

“Yeah… It is… Do you read often?”

“I read a lot, actually. That’s one of the major differences between me and Aiden.”

“… You seem introverted, while he’s extroverted”

“Yeah… I guess that’s the case”

Then Ethan got up to get a book, then came back to the chair

They both read in silence, but it wasn’t awkward

It was just… Quiet.
