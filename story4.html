A World of Angels and Demons

Chapter 4

By the time Marin got home, heather wasn’t around.

“… she’s probably at the bar again…” He muttered to himself, before going to his room

When he checked his phone, his eyes widened slightly

“… 20 unread messages… From Aiden …”

He clicked on it, opening up all the texts

All of them were just to check up on him

He sighed when he saw the last few texts

‘I’m boredddddd’

‘Marin?????’

‘Glowstick???????’

‘Why aren’t you respondingggggggg?’

‘Are you seeing my texts?????’

‘HELLO??????’

‘☹️?????’

After reading them, he slowly typed out…

‘Hey Aiden. Thanks for the boba, I really appreciate it. How’d you know I’d like strawberry?’

Aiden replied in under 10 seconds

‘FINALLY! I have been waiting for you to come online for two whole HOURS ☹️! Anyways, hey. You’re welcome. And… I guess I kinda thought strawberry kinda matched your whole vibe.‘

Marin couldn’t help the small smile that crept up on his lips at the text. He replied…

‘Sorry for keeping you waiting, I just had a lot of stuff to do today’

‘Really?’

‘Yeah’

‘So…WYD rn?’

‘Nothing much, just lying in bed’

‘K’

‘What about you?’

‘I’m doing homework. While eating, and watching Netflix’

‘Sounds like you’re really busy’

‘Nah. It’s fine, but is it alr if I ask you a question?’

Marin tensed at the implication.

He pondered over what Aiden possibly wanted to ask, before he replied

‘Go ahead…’

After a whole minute passed without Aiden replying, Marin started to internally panic.

Till the message eventually dropped.

‘I don’t know why, but I just feel like there’s something off about you. I just wanted to know if you’re REALLY ok….’

Marin’s heart practically stopped at the question.

Did Aiden know?

How much did he know?

Did Marin not hide his scars properly?

Marin’s brain was going 200 miles a minute.

He felt heat rise to his throat.

With shaking hands, he typed

‘I’m fine, why do you ask?’

Marin wanted to throw up. Usually, he didn’t mind lying to protect himself, but this time…?

It felt extremely wrong.

With his phone still in his hand, he bolted for the bathroom.

He couldn’t help it as the bile made its way out of his body

When he rinsed his face, he took a long hard look at himself in the mirror.

He didn’t know why the thought of people knowing what was wrong made him sick.

It just did.

He went back to his room, and sat on the edge of his bed.

He checked his phone, looking at the text Aiden sent

‘I just wanted to make sure you’re ok. I care. I…keep getting this weird feeling that there’s something off, or just…wrong. It’s ok if you don’t want to talk about it, just…you’re not alone, ok?’

Something in Marin's chest clenched.

Aiden was so...kind. so...nice.

Marin considered telling the truth

But quickly stopped himself.

'what if I tell him...and I become a burden to him...? Or worse...what if he leaves...?' Marin thought to himself, sighing as he looked at the message.

'thanks for caring, Aiden. But I'm fine, really.' he typed out with trembling fingers.

'ok, if you say so. Anyways, I GTG, see you later?' Aiden replied

'see you later'

'bye ✌️'

'bye'

Marin sighed, collapsing back onto his bed.

'youre not alone'

The words echoed in his head like a siren song.

Aiden noticed him.

All Marin ever wanted...was to be noticed.

But now..?

He's terrified.
